1. What is DeFi?¶

Computer in the financial sky

  • Blockchain →\to→ the computer in the sky.

  • DeFi →\to→ the set of financial applications running on the blockchain machine.

Definition

Decentralized Finance (DeFi) is an open digital ecosystem where financial services are produced through automated smart contracts in order to eliminate financial intermediation.

DeFi inherits Blockchain properties:

  • A set of public, interoperable and autonomous protocols which are universally accessible

  • Developed, maintained and used by an open pool of pseudonymous agents rather than a set of unique legal entities.

4. The future of DeFi¶

The fundamental challenge

Recall the binary information structure

  • Public (on-chain)

    • Verification at (almost) zero cost

    • Any activity on DeFi is public and can be contracted upon.

  • Private (off-chain)

    • Infinitely costly to verify

    • Pseudonymity: identity-related information cannot be contracted upon in DeFi

→\to→ ∃ \exists~∃  limit on the contracting space and the scope of applications for DeFi protocols.

The smart contract challenge (Gans, 2022)

Verification vs Contracting power

$\to$ Growth perspective for DeFi?

$\to$ Value of DeFi for the real economy?

The new intermediaries¶

The solution to expanding contracting power is to introduce new forms of intermediation: information bridges

  • Oracles

  • Ramps

  • Token issuers (linked to off-chain)

Trade-off: verification vs contracting power

Oracles¶

Definition

Oracles provide the necessary bridge to unify off-chain and on-chain worlds

  • Service that connects smart contracts with external data sources.

Oracles expand the contracting space of DeFi applications at the cost of on-chain (public) verification.

Applications

  1. Price Feeds
    Oracles fetch and deliver real-time price data for assets to DeFi platforms
    ex: cryptocurrencies, commodities
    application: liquidation thresholds

  2. External Event Verification
    Oracles confirm external events
    ex: weather, politics, sports
    application: derivatives, prediction markets, insurance

  3. Cross-Chain Communication
    Oracles facilitate interactions between different blockchains, making cross-chain DeFi products possible.

Ramps & Tokenisation¶

Definition

Tokenization refers to the process of generating a digital representation of traditional assets on a blockchain by on and off ramps.

FSB(2023)

Benefits

  • Automation and trade speed & efficiency

  • New contracting opportunities: information space and composability

Applications

  • Stablecoins & cross-border payments
    Bypass banks (correspondent) and message platforms (swift)

  • Foreign exchanges
    Payment vs payment

  • Mortgage-backed-securities
    Dozen intermediaries in the process

Tokenization continuum¶

Not all digital assets offer the same tokenization value:

  • Worse candidates: syndicated loans or commercial real estate.

  • Better candidates: fiat currencies, FX or MBS

Challenges¶

The private and social costs of unverifiable information

  • Centralization
    Economies of scale/scope →\to→ cf: Market power, Too-big-to-fail

  • Inefficiency
    Oracles information rents →\to→ cf: Credit rating agencies, market benchmarks

  • Vulnerabilities
    Token redemption (stablecoin) →\to→ cf: Runs on mutual funds and banks
    Oracle manipulation →\to→ cf: Audit collusion, corruption

  • Liabilities
    Legal challenges: who has what right? →\to→ cf: Consumer protection, prospectus issuance

Opportunities for public interventions →\to→ cf: traditional market failures in information markets

  • Public provision

  • Licensed actors

  • Regulated oracle/token markets
    →\to→ cf: MICA and GENIUS ACT

Policy discussion¶

Regulatory regimes built around intermediaries as regulated processors of transaction information may fit poorly with a disintermediated market structure.

WEF (2021)

A new policy framework?¶

Should DeFi receive a different policy treatment?

↳ How do DeFi services differ in their treatment of information frictions?

Claim: Understanding the shift in information structure sheds light on

  • the scope of DeFi applications

  • risks and inefficiencies (new and old)

  • appropriate policy approaches (warranted and feasible actions)

Elements of reflection

  • Limits on policy enforcement power and information acquisition

    • Computer in the sky: no shutting down, no liability, etc.

  • New targets: validators, protocols, issuers and oracles.

  • Eligible proposals: warranted & feasible

    • Warranted
      Several frictions can be best addressed by the private sector
      Find cases with limits to the production of private solutions
      ↳ likely benefits from public support.

    • Feasible
      Subset of warranted actions satisfying the technological constraints of DeFi

Open policy issues¶

  • How much decentralisation is good for the economy?
    Technology alone is no guarantee against centralisation of DeFi

  • How to enforce liabilities in a pseudonymous ecosystem? (public-private issue)
    Ensure AML-KYC standards

  • How to manage unverifiable information? (public-private issue)
    Embedding off chain information introduces a new information friction
    Policing oracles and ramps →\to→ Recovering the value of traditional frameworks

  • How to think about macro-prudential regimes in absence of enforcement powers?
    Interconnected protocols - Misaligned incentives to audit - Contagion to the real economy

Conclusion¶

The main value proposition offered by DeFi is a shift in the governance of information.

  • Address social costs associated with financial intermediation

  • Introduce its own set of social costs (new and old)

For the crypto enthusiasts

  • Substituting automated protocols and pseudonymity for the confidentiality of financial intermediaries does not fully remove the fundamental information frictions at the heart of financial contracts

For policymakers

  • Derive policy approaches that are both warranted and feasible under the technological constraints of DeFi

  • Investigate how the treatment of information differs under different DeFi protocols
    →\to→ Design appropriate approaches for public support and intervention
